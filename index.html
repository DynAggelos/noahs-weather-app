Noah Weather App

Instructions

Basic Usage

To see upcoming weather straight from Weather.gov (i.e., NOAA), either:

  • type in the coordinates (longitude, latitude) you want to see the weather for into the coordinates textbox below, or
  • press the Use Browser Coordinates button and allow the page to access your location to put your general coordinates in the textbox.

When the textbox contains valid longitude and latitude coordinates, click Submit Coordinates to see the weather.

Saving Coordinates

To save your coordinates for future use, make sure they are entered properly into the coordinates box, press Save These Coordinates, and then bookmark the page after it reloads. Then every time you use the bookmark, the coordinates you "saved" will be automatically loaded into the coordinates textbox for you.

About Letting Your Browser Access Your Location

This feature uses your IP address to determine your general location. If you have your WiFi on, any WiFi hotspots around you will also probably be used to get a much more exact location. If you don't want to send Weather.gov your exact location (which really is unnecessary anyway), keep your WiFi off when you press the Use Browser Coordinates button. More information about how the Firefox browser determines your location when you allow it to is found here. Please note that your coordinates are not saved by this app in any way, although your IP address and coordinates may be saved by Repl.it and/or Weather.gov, according to their privacy policies on user data retention.

This app uses (will use) the US cities database from simplemaps.com/data/us-cities
